Email Domain Inbox Summary runs in Copilot in Outlook: pass a domain and a timeframe, get back a table—who emailed, when, what action is needed, and whether anyone replied. The "forgot to follow up" risk for account managers and PMs becomes a 1-minute morning check.
🎁 What will you get?
🔎 Scope to one customer
Filter by domain so 100 newsletter notifications don't drown the signal.
✅ Action column
Each row spells out what's expected of you—no more re-reading threads.
↩️ Replied column
Flags whether anyone on your side responded. Dropped customer emails: gone.
🚀 Copy This Prompt (English Version)
Create a tableshowing all emails I received from DOMAINNAME (e.g., @customer.com) in the last X weeks. Include a column that shows any action required from the message and another column to show if anyone at my company has responded yet.
📋 提示詞範本（繁體中文版）
建立一張表格，列出我在過去 X 週內從 DOMAINNAME（例如 @customer.com）收到的所有 email。請包含一欄顯示這封信需要哪些行動，以及另一欄顯示我公司是否有人已經回覆。
💡 Three Ways to Use It
1
📧 Open Copilot in Outlook
Desktop or mobile—just paste the prompt straight in.
2
✏️ Replace DOMAINNAME and X weeks
Plug in the real customer domain (e.g. @chunghwa.com.tw) and the window you care about (1, 2, 4, 8…).
3
🎯 Triage "not replied" first
That column is today's must-do; for replied rows, verify the response and whether to follow up.
